Work

ZTC Pathway Mapper

A production-ready web application for tracking Zero Textbook Cost (ZTC) course adoption across academic pathways.

Built to transform CSV data into interactive visual dashboards with comprehensive export capabilities. Features include:

  • Visual Progress Tracking: Color-coded dashboards with real-time statistics
  • Smart Filtering: Filter by term, status, and course search
  • Detailed Analytics: Drill-down into course-level ZTC metrics
  • Professional Exports: Filter-aware PDF, CSV, and PNG reports
  • ADA Compliant: WCAG 2.1 accessibility standards
  • Zero Cost: 100% client-side, no server required

Technologies: React 18, Tailwind CSS, Chart.js, jsPDF, SheetJS
Status: Production deployment, UAT approved

About

I am Bradley Franklin, Administrator of Instructional Systems and Pathway Innovation in the Institutional Effectiveness department at Chaffey College, where I bridge the gap between higher education administration and technology innovation.

My current focus centers on Zero Textbook Cost (ZTC) initiatives, where I develop data visualization tools and analytical frameworks to track course adoption patterns, measure program impact, and support institutional decision-making. This work involves processing complex datasets across multiple academic pathways (Cal-GETC, Communication Studies) and ZTC classification types (ZTCA, ZTCC, ZTCE, ZTCF, ZTCG, LCT), transforming raw institutional data into actionable intelligence.

I bring a unique combination of administrative insight and technical capability to every project. My approach emphasizes user-centered design, accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1), and creating production-ready solutions that serve both immediate operational needs and long-term strategic planning.

Recent highlights include developing the ZTC Pathway Mapper—a comprehensive web application featuring real-time data filtering, multi-format export capabilities, and ADA-compliant design. This project evolved from proof-of-concept to a portfolio-quality tool that demonstrates both technical proficiency and practical institutional impact.

Beyond my professional role, I am pursuing doctoral studies where I continue exploring the intersection of educational technology, data analytics, and institutional effectiveness. My work reflects a commitment to leveraging technology to enhance educational access, affordability, and student success.
